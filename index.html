About Me

Hi! I'm Emily, an AI/ML and data science specialist passionate about AI sustainability and energy consumption. I will be joining Microsoft as a Product Manager in the AI Development Acceleration Program (MAIDAP) in July 2025. Currently, I am pursuing my Master's in Computer Science at Cornell Tech, with a strong focus on AI applications and scalable machine learning systems.

I graduated summa cum laude from Providence College with a B.S. in Computer Science and minors in Data Science, Mathematics, and Political Science. I also participated in an exchange program at DIS Copenhagen, where I explored AI sustainability from a European perspective.

My professional experience spans multiple industries and global locations. At Millennium, I worked as a Quantitative Research Intern, leveraging AI models for market trends. At Procter & Gamble, I was an IT Product Management Intern leading digital transformations. At Louis Dreyfus Company, I specialized in remote sensing and AI-powered commodities trading. My international work in Geneva, Switzerland and my study abroad in Denmark gave me a unique global perspective on AI and sustainability.

Providence College & Academic Honors

Phi Beta Kappa

Membership in Phi Beta Kappa is by invitation and requires excellence in liberal arts and sciences, completion of at least 90 credit hours in these fields, intermediate-level proficiency in a second language, and completion of a mathematics, logic, or statistics course.

Learn more about my Phi Beta Kappa induction

Sigma Xi

Sigma Xi is an international honor society for science and engineering, recognizing excellence in research. More than 200 Nobel Prize winners have been members.

Pi Sigma Alpha

Pi Sigma Alpha is the national political science honor society. Members must complete at least ten semester-credits in political science, maintain a B average, and be in the top one-third of their class.

Upsilon Pi Epsilon

The international honor society for computing and information disciplines, UPE membership requires students to rank in the top 35% of their class, have a GPA of at least 3.0, and complete at least 45 semester hours of coursework in computing-related subjects.

Summa Cum Laude

Summa cum laude is the highest academic distinction at Providence College, awarded to students with a cumulative GPA between 3.85 and 4.00. View the full list of 2024 Latin Honors recipients at Providence College Commencement.

My Courses at Cornell

Fall 2024
